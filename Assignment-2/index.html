Earthquakes Recorded Between 1995 and 2023

An earthquake is what happens when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane. The location below the earth's surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

The Ring of Fire, also referred to as the Circum-Pacific Belt, is a path along the Pacific Ocean characterized by active volcanoes and frequent earthquakes. The majority of Earth's volcanoes and earthquakes take place along the Ring of Fire.

Warnings and Measurement Methods

The alert colour system used with earthqaukes is categorise the shaking instensity of the quake. For example green (35.3%) means no damge but weakly felt, yellow (5.9%) means potential damage and weakly felt, orange (2.4%) means perception of severe shaking and potentially moderate damage, and finally red (1.3%) means extreme shaking and severe damage, and in the chart black (55.1%) signified earthquakes that received no alert at all.

Mw (Moment magnitude), Mwb (body wave), Mwc (centroid), and Mww (Moment W-phase) are all primary methods of measuring an earthquakes magnitude. The selected four types are the best at measuring how earthquakes specifically act when they are occurring and allow the correct interpretation by professionals on how communities and those affected should act as a response.
