Earthquakes Recorded Between 1995 and 2023

An earthquake is what happens when two blocks of the earth suddenly slip past one another. The surface where they slip is called the fault or fault plane. The location below the earth's surface where the earthquake starts is called the hypocenter, and the location directly above it on the surface of the earth is called the epicenter.

The Ring of Fire, also referred to as the Circum-Pacific Belt, is a path along the Pacific Ocean characterized by active volcanoes and frequent earthquakes. The majority of Earth's volcanoes and earthquakes take place along the Ring of Fire.

As shown above, most earthquakes occur in land based regions due to the tectonic plates and fault lines.

Additionally, it is recorded that most of the the earthquakes recorded are being done so over small islands or locations not part of a larger continent.

Warnings, Measurement Methods and Significance

The alert colour system used with earthqaukes is categorise the shaking instensity of the quake. For example green means no damge but weakly felt, yellow means potential damage and weakly felt, orange means perception of severe shaking and potentially moderate damage, and finally red means extreme shaking and severe damage.

Since 1995, 55.1% of earthquakes received no alert all, 35.3% received a green alert, 5.9% received a yellow alert, 2.4% received a orange alert, 1.3% received a red alert

Mw (Moment magnitude), Mwb (body wave), Mwc (centroid), and Mww (Moment W-phase) are all methods of measuring an earthquakes magnitude.
